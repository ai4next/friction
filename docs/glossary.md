# Glossary

> Formal definitions of all Friction terminology.

---

### Challenge

A structured objection generated by a persona, targeted at a specific assumption, goal, or constraint in the founder's plan. Each challenge has a severity (blocker / major / minor / informational), a headline, a detailed body, optional evidence, and optional counter-proposal.

**Example**: "Your assumption that Twitter is your primary channel conflicts with your Friday deadline — Twitter campaigns need 3-5 days of lead time."

---

### Decision Debt

Unresolved challenges that accumulate over time and resurface in future planning sessions. Debt accrues through: (1) deferring a challenge, (2) rejecting a high-severity challenge with weak rationale, or (3) making contradictory decisions. Decision debt is the system's memory of what the founder chose not to address.

**For example**: Deferring a pricing assumption is decision debt. Six months later when revenue is below projections, the system surfaces this: "You deferred 3 pricing decisions. Your current pricing may be the cause."

---

### Decision Engine

The interactive component that presents challenges to the founder one at a time and records their responses (approve / modify / reject / defer / skip). It manages the flow of review, tracks the founder's rationale, and determines when debt should accrue.

---

### Escalation

The process by which decision debt entries automatically increase in priority as they age without resolution. Rules-based: blocker debt older than 30 days becomes unskippable; any debt older than 60 days gets a "CRITICAL" tag.

---

### Explicit Assumption

An assumption the founder stated directly in their input.

**Example**: Founder says "Twitter is our primary marketing channel." → Explicit assumption: "Twitter will drive the most signups."

---

### Extracted Assumption

An assumption that is implicitly present in the founder's plan but was never directly stated. These are identified by the Specification Layer and are considered high-value targets for the Red-Team because they represent the founder's unconscious biases.

**Example**: Founder says "We'll launch on Product Hunt." → Extracted assumption: "Our target audience browses Product Hunt and is open to trying new tools there."

---

### Persona

An AI agent with a distinct perspective, system prompt, and questioning strategy. The four built-in personas are: Devil's Advocate, Domain Expert, Customer Skeptic, and Technical Debt Guardian. Founders can create custom personas.

---

### Persona Compiler

An LLM-powered component that converts a founder's natural-language persona description into a structured system prompt matching the same format as built-in personas. Enabled founders to create custom critique perspectives without prompt engineering.

---

### Pattern Alert

A system-generated insight that identifies recurring decision patterns across sessions. Triggered when the same assumption is challenged and deferred/rejected 3+ times, or when related debt entries form a thematic cluster.

**Example**: "You've rejected retention-related challenges 4 times. Your churn data suggests these concerns were valid."

---

### Plan

The structured output of the Specification Layer, containing the founder's goals, assumptions (both explicit and extracted), constraints, and acceptance criteria. The Plan is the input to the Red-Team Layer and the foundation of all challenges.

---

### Red-Team Layer

Layer 2 of the Friction system. Receives the structured Plan from the Specification Layer and dispatches it to all active personas in parallel. Each persona independently generates challenges. The layer also handles deduplication and conflict detection across persona outputs.

---

### Session

A single interaction with Friction, from input through challenge review to completion or abandonment. Sessions track state through a defined state machine (INIT → SPECIFYING → SPEC_COMPLETE → RED_TEAMING → CHALLENGES_READY → REVIEWING → COMPLETED / ABANDONED). Incomplete sessions can be resumed.

---

### Specification Layer

Layer 1 of the Friction system. Converts raw founder input (voice or text) into a structured Plan via a two-pass LLM process: extraction followed by verification. Makes implicit assumptions explicit.

---

### Structured Plan

See **Plan**.

---

### Devil's Advocate

A persona that identifies failure modes by inverting assumptions, finding single points of failure, and tracing failure cascades. The most general-purpose persona, useful in every session.

---

### Domain Expert

A persona that injects industry-specific knowledge (benchmarks, regulations, competitive dynamics, failure patterns). Configured per project with a domain (e.g., "B2B SaaS," "healthcare"). Most valuable when the founder is new to the domain.

---

### Customer Skeptic

A persona representing the least-enthusiastic, most-demanding customer. Focuses on value proposition, onboarding friction, switching costs, and the question "Why would I switch from what I already use?"

---

### Technical Debt Guardian

A persona that evaluates technical assumptions for scalability, testability, observability, and migration paths. Distinguishes between good debt (intentional, tracked) and bad debt (invisible, compounding). Provides equally-fast alternatives that don't accumulate debt.

---

### Tiered Context

The system's strategy for managing context window limits: Tier 1 (always: system prompt + current plan), Tier 2 (if space: project summary + recent decisions), Tier 3 (on demand: full history). Ensures persona calls stay within context limits while preserving relevant context.

---

### Two-Pass Extraction

The Specification Layer's approach: Pass 1 extracts assumptions/goals/constraints from raw input; Pass 2 verifies the extraction for completeness and accuracy. Detects hallucinations before the Red-Team runs.

---

### Waterfall Mode

An alternative to the default parallel persona execution. In waterfall mode, later personas receive the output of earlier personas. Used when a dependency exists between personas (e.g., Domain Expert's findings should inform Technical Debt Guardian's analysis).

---

### Conflict Detection

A post-processing step that identifies direct contradictions between challenges from different personas. Contradictions are tagged as `conflict_pair` and presented together to the founder, who must resolve the tension.

---

### Debate Mode

An optional, founder-triggered mode where two conflicting personas engage in a structured back-and-forth (3 rounds max). Each persona sees the other's argument and responds. The debate transcript is appended to the Plan for the founder's final decision.

---

### Challenge Severity

A classification of a challenge's importance:
- **Blocker**: If valid, the plan cannot proceed without addressing this
- **Major**: If valid, the plan needs significant adjustment
- **Minor**: If valid, the plan needs minor tweaks
- **Informational**: A note worth considering, no action required

---

### Append-Only Decision Log

An immutable record of every decision the founder makes during a session. Stores challenge ID, action taken, rationale, and whether debt was accrued. Used for pattern analysis and audit trail.

---

### Local-First

The system's default deployment model: all data stays on the founder's machine. SQLite for storage, local Whisper for voice transcription, no external telemetry. Cloud deployment is an optional future path.
